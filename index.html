Piedmont Healthcare: Cybersecurity Threat Landscape

Understanding threats, threat actors, and defenses in Southeastern U.S. healthcare

Overview of Piedmont Healthcare

Piedmont Healthcare is a not-for-profit healthcare system serving Georgia with over a dozen hospitals and hundreds of locations. As a major healthcare provider, it's a high-value target for cybercriminals.

Primary Cyber Threats

  • Ransomware attacks (e.g., Ryuk, Conti, LockBit)
  • Phishing campaigns targeting employees
  • Data exfiltration of PHI (Protected Health Information)
  • Exploitation of outdated medical IoT devices

Notable Incidents

2020 Universal Health Services ransomware attack: Took down systems across 400+ U.S. facilities, highlighting vulnerabilities in healthcare IT.

Threat Actors & Motivations

Groups like Conti and LockBit are known for targeting healthcare due to its critical nature and likelihood to pay ransom. Many operate from Eastern Europe and are financially motivated. Nation-state actors may also target health orgs for data.

Attack Vectors

  • Phishing emails (initial access)
  • Stolen credentials from prior breaches
  • Unpatched vulnerabilities in hospital software
  • Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) exploitation

Defensive Technologies Used

  • Endpoint detection and response (e.g., SentinelOne)
  • Email filtering and anti-phishing training
  • Network segmentation and firewalls
  • Encryption of sensitive data
  • Backup and recovery systems (air-gapped backups)

Standards & Compliance

Piedmont must comply with HIPAA, NIST guidelines, and HITECH Act. These standards help ensure PHI protection, security audits, and response planning.

Business Implications

  • Downtime impacts emergency care and patient safety
  • Fines for HIPAA violations
  • Reputational harm and patient trust erosion
  • Ransom payments and recovery costs in millions
