Piedmont Healthcare Cybersecurity Threat Landscape

Protecting critical infrastructure and patient data in Georgia's healthcare system

About Piedmont Healthcare

Piedmont Healthcare is a leading nonprofit system in Georgia, operating hospitals and clinics serving millions. Its size and responsibility make it a prime cyber target.

Top Threats

  • Ransomware attacks (Conti, LockBit, etc.)
  • Phishing emails and credential harvesting
  • Medical IoT exploitation (unpatched devices)
  • Data breaches targeting PHI/PII

Real Incidents

UHS Ransomware Incident (2020): Disrupted care at 400+ facilities, revealing system-wide vulnerabilities.

Threat Actors & Their Motives

Ransomware gangs like LockBit and Conti, mostly from Eastern Europe, target healthcare for fast payouts. Nation-state actors may target PHI for espionage or leverage during crises.

Attack Vectors

  • Phishing (email entry point)
  • Exposed RDP ports
  • Stolen credentials from the dark web
  • Unpatched PACS or EHR systems

Defensive Technologies

  • Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR) like SentinelOne
  • Zero Trust architecture and MFA
  • SIEM tools and 24/7 SOC monitoring
  • Encrypted, offline backups
  • Incident Response Plans (IRP)

Regulatory Requirements

Piedmont must comply with HIPAA, NIST Cybersecurity Framework, and HITECH Act regulations. These ensure secure handling of electronic health records and breach notification protocols.

Business Impact

  • Downtime affecting emergency services
  • Fines and legal repercussions
  • Damage to brand reputation
  • Cost of recovery often exceeds ransom
